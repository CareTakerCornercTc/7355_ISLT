Healthcare: Types of Policies


Introduction to Healthcare Policies


It is quite possible that you have not read or even know what your loved one has for health insurance. I know that you are dealing with a lot of emotions right now but it is important that you find out what health insurance coverage or coverages they have because it will help determine what types of care they qualify for under the terms of their policies.


It will also contain duration of treatment types, medications that may or may not covered, preauthorization for certain treatments and other important details. You should make a copy of the policy. Benefits: Other Types of Coverage


Introduction to Other Types of Benefits


Another thing that you might not be aware of is that in these circumstances, your loved one may have other benefits or insurance coverage that can assist with their needs in this time of crisis. For example, if your loved one is a veteran or the spouse of a veteran, they may be entitled to certain veteran benefits such as Aid & Attendance.


It is possible that they are covered under long term disability insurance that may cover some of the potential costs to care for them. Hopefully, you know where this information is or that your loved one can communicate it to you.


If not, take a deep breath, remain calm and begin looking for the information. Remember, it is possible that there is no other coverage.




Types of Living Arrangements


What Is Safe Now?


In this new reality, living arrangements may happen in several different stages before a more permanent placement occurs. For example, a stroke victim may stay full-time temporarily at a rehabilitation center (which is usually part of a nursing home) but a separate building or floor. Once rehabilitation is completed based on the insurance coverage, a decision will be made as to where the stroke victim will be placed.


If they cannot care for themselves, they must have a suitable person care for them or be placed in a nursing home/facility that can accommodate their needs. The level of care at the nursing home depends on the level of their disability or how well they can take care of themselves. If they cannot prepare their own meals, shower, go to the bathroom without help, or take medications, then they would be placed in the highest level of care which is skilled nursing. If they can take care of some of their basic needs then they may be placed in unskilled nursing which means that they are somewhat independent and able to meet some of their own basic needs without help.
